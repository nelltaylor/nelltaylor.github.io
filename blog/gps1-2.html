What is Pair Programming?

Pair programming is the process of two people trying to solve a challenge, like a feature, challenge, or piece of software. It's a collaborative process where two people share ideas and approach a problem as driver and navigator.

Why is it Important to DBC?

Besides being a common industry practice and job skill in agile development — especially in Ruby on Rails — Pair Programming is important to the culture aspects that DBC fosters in its students including the encouragement of:

  • Collaboration
  • Peer learning & mentoring
  • Reviewing your code early and often (as is the Chicago way).

Driver's Role

  • At the keyboard
  • Actually writing the code

Navigator's Role

  • Steering
  • Reviewing
  • Big picture
